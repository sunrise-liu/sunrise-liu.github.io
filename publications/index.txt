We study sharp RD settings in which manipulation takes the form of a one-sided, nonnegative shift in the running variable concentrated in a right-sided neighborhood of the threshold. Our target is a well-defined causal parameter: the RD treatment effect at the cutoff among nonmanipulators. We propose a two-stage de-manipulated RD estimator. In the first stage, we learn the conditional mean of the running variable given immutable covariates using an auxiliary sample outside the suspicious region and form residuals within the suspicious region. We then classify manipulators via a tuning-light Mean-Gap Split on ordered residuals. In the second stage, we apply standard local-polynomial RD methods with robust bias correction to the predicted nonmanipulator subsample. We establish large-sample conditions under which the split consistently estimates the manipulator share and the recovered set of nonmanipulators is consistent (and exact under a hardseparation condition).
